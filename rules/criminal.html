CRIMINAL RP RULES
Crime's encouraged. Chaos isn't. Know the difference.

The Spirit of Criminal RP

Criminal RP is some of the most fun you can have on this server. Heists, gangs, drug operations, turf wars — it's all on the table. But it only works when criminals play with purpose, not just to grief. A good criminal leaves a mark on the city. A bad one just makes everyone log off.

1. Initiation & Setup

  • You must verbally initiate before using violence. 'Get on the ground or I shoot' is an initiation. Running up and punching someone is not. 
  • Initiation applies to your group. If Player A initiates with Player B's crew, the whole crew is engaged — but so is yours. There are limits on player counts in combat, see combat rules.
  • You cannot shoot someone you've never interacted with IC. Build the story first.
  • Birdy messages alone (without prior RP) are not a valid initiation for lethal force.(birdy beef is not valid initiation)
  • Repeating the same criminal activity is weak RP, try and keep things fresh.
  • Illegal Dealer mission areas are considered “Red Zones” for the duration of the mission and DO NOT require initiation.

2. Robberies

  • Player robberies: you can rob players of cash, items, and vehicles with proper RP.  DO NOT pocket wipe i.e. removing phones, removing food and drinks.
  • Player robberies are permitted, but within reason, If you are being targeted excessively to the point of harassment put in a ticket.
  • There is a system in place to show which heists are permitted based on PD numbers and cooldowns. Hit the home key to see.

3. Hostage Rules

  • Hostages must be treated as people, not furniture. Engage with them. Give them a reason to be scared.
  • You cannot kill a hostage unless negotiations have fully broken down and you've given fair warning.
  • Respect your hostages time, they are players too.
  • Don't take the same person hostage repeatedly — it's not fun for them and it's lazy RP.

4. Gangs & Turf

  • Gang membership must be roleplayed — not just declaring yourself a gang with no backstory or structure. To start a gang you must open a gang ticket, gang staff will assist and give you all relevant information.
  • Gangs must be recognised by the HIGH TABLE to be official.
  • Turf wars need IC history. You can't declare war on a gang, if you have no RP reason to declare war.
  • Drive-bys on rival gang territory require a prior escalation chain.
  • We do not allow gang impersonation.
  • Gangs who wish to have compounds and MLO’s must purchase that through the Tebex package.
  • There is a gang bible, it is available to all whitelisted players.

5. Drugs & Contraband

  • Drug manufacturing and selling is an active RP process — you don't just press a button. Create scenes around supply, demand, and risk.
  • Drug spots are contestable IC. If someone muscled you off a corner, deal with it in-character. 

6. Rule of Engagement — When Things Go Wrong

  • If you're caught, you're caught. Accept the RP consequence. Remember to value your life. Role play over gun play. Take the loss, and remember that both sides of the scene are players.
  • Being flat backed during a criminal operation triggers NLR — you don't remember the heist, the crew, or who shot you, the scene in general. Unless you are taken to the hospital, or picked up by EMS then the memory must be fuzzy.
  • Escalation must be proportionate. A verbal insult doesn't usually justify a murder. Build up the tension.

7. Cop Baiting

  • Deliberately trying to start a police chase with zero meaningful RP is considered baiting and will be handled if need be.
  • Doing donuts outside the PD, honking at officers, or dressing as a cop to confuse dispatch are all examples of low-effort baiting.
  • If you want a chase, set up a real scenario. It'll be way more fun for everyone.

The best criminals are the ones cops love to catch. Make the city earn its crime stories.
