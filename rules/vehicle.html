VEHICLE RULES
Drive like there's a cop watching. Because there probably is.

Why Vehicle Rules Matter

Cars are one of the most common sources of RP issues on any server. Whether it's a pursuit, a crash, or just parking on someone's lawn, this doc covers what's expected behind the wheel.

1. Drive Realistically

  • Drive at speeds that make sense for the road and situation. Nobody in real life is doing 200mph through downtown.
  • Obey traffic laws when there's no active RP reason not to. Red lights, stop signs, lane discipline — it all matters.
  • Don't treat the city like a racetrack just because you're bored. It kills immersion for everyone around you.
  • Off-road in off-road vehicles. Don't take a Lambo through the Alamo Sea.

2. Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM)

  • Do NOT use your vehicle as a weapon.
  • Hit-and-runs on players require active, ongoing RP justification.
  • Ramming police vehicles during a chase must have a proportionate in-character reason.
  • Pinning someone on foot with a vehicle to rob/detain them is NOT allowed.

3. Vehicle Pursuits

  • Pursuit rules for LEOs are defined in the LEO SOP. Civilians: use good judgment and don't intentionally bait chases.
  • Helicopter griefing (hovering over a scene just to be annoying) is a violation.

4. Storing & Spawning Vehicles

  • Don't spawn or de-spawn vehicles in the middle of active RP scenes.
  • Vehicles left in a public space are subject to being towed by police or mechanics.
  • Garages are the only valid spawn location for personal vehicles. No summoning a car from thin air in an alley.
  • Don't de-spawn your car to avoid it being impounded — that is power gaming and will be treated accordingly.

5. Illegal Modifications

  • Custom illegal modifications (nitrous, illegal plates, weapons mounts) exist in-lore but require IC acquisition through the right channels.
  • Cosmetic mods are fine — go wild with the wrap.

6. Emergency Vehicles

  • Civilian players cannot drive emergency vehicles (police cars, ambulances, fire trucks) without whitelisted faction approval.
  • If you steal an emergency vehicle for RP purposes, it must be part of a real scene — not just joyriding in a cop car.
  • Yield to emergency vehicles with lights and sirens. Yes, in-character.

7. Aircraft

  • Flying requires a pilot's license obtained through in-game means.
  • Don't fly over active police scenes, robberies, or chases to stream-snipe or relay info. That's metagaming.
  • Restricted airspace (military base, prison, etc.) is off-limits without specific RP authorization.
  • Crash-landing on someone is still VDM. Yes, even in a plane.

Your car is an extension of your character — treat it like one. A wheelman has a rep. Don't blow it on a random.
